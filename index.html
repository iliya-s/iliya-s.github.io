Computational Scientist

Focused on modeling and understanding complex systems.

First and foremost, I think of myself as a scientist—driven by curiosity, respect for the underlying structure of a problem, and a desire to understand how things actually work. Computers are my primary tool, not as an end in themselves, but as a way to build and test models of complex phenomena. This has been a constant theme in my work: from studying strongly correlated systems in electronic structure theory during my PhD, to modeling cross-asset valuation adjustments in derivatives trading today. The domains have changed, but the objective remains the same—understand the system, and build frameworks that capture its behavior.
