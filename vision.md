# Website vision

## Core idea

This site should feel like a clean public record of serious work.

Not a personal brand machine.
Not a startup landing page.
Not an online diary.

It should say:

**I build real things. I think clearly. I work on hard problems.**

The tone should be direct, restrained, and confident.

## What the site is for

The site has four jobs:

1. Show technical credibility
2. Make your work legible
3. Show range without feeling scattered
4. Give people a clean way to understand how you think

## Positioning

You are not just "a quant" and not just "a builder."
The site should present you as:

**Quantitative researcher and builder working on derivatives, trading systems, and decision-making under uncertainty.**

That line is broad enough to hold your job, your side projects, and your writing.

## Design direction

The design should be minimal.

- dark text on an off-white or white background, or the reverse
- strong typography
- generous spacing
- no noisy gradients
- no flashy animations
- no jargon-heavy blocks
- no long mission statements

It should feel closer to a well-designed paper or notebook than a startup site.

## Voice

The writing should be simple and direct.

Use:
- Built
- Designed
- Developed
- Researched
- Focused on
- Working on

Avoid:
- passionate about
- world-class
- innovative solutions
- leveraging synergies
- overly poetic copy on the main pages

The language should feel controlled and precise.

## Site map

Keep version 1 small.

### Home
A short statement of who you are and what you work on.

### Projects
The center of gravity of the site.
Each project should be short, concrete, and legible.

### Experience
A clean summary of professional experience.

### Writing
Optional, but useful.
A few short pieces are enough.

## Homepage vision

The home page should answer three questions immediately:

1. Who are you?
2. What do you work on?
3. Where should I go next?

Suggested structure:

- Name
- One-line descriptor
- One short paragraph
- Buttons for Projects, Experience, Resume
- A small featured work section below

## Projects vision

Projects should be written like this:

### Horizon
Systematic macro ETF trading framework.

- Forecasts cross-sectional returns
- Constructs portfolios under risk and turnover constraints
- Evaluates performance with walk-forward testing

### Arena
Sports prediction market engine.

- Estimates live probabilities with state-space models
- Replays historical market states for testing
- Designed for research and execution in prediction markets

The key is that each project should say what it is, what it does, and why it matters.

## Experience vision

This page should be professional and compressed.

Example:

### Wells Fargo — Securities Quantitative Analytics, VP
- Built interest-rate and cross-asset pricing models
- Worked on XVA and structured products
- Developed simulation and valuation tools for front-office workflows

Keep it clean. No autobiographical detours.

## Writing vision

Writing should be thoughtful but disciplined.

Good subjects:
- forecasting versus portfolio construction
- regime change in trading systems
- what makes a research problem worth pursuing
- useful models versus elegant models

The writing page should not feel like a journal. It should feel curated.

## Overall feeling

Someone should leave the site thinking:

**This person has technical depth, clear taste, and real initiative.**

That is the standard.
